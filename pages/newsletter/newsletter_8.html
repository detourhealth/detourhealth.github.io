INNOVATION PEARL

Needs screening: disease state fundamentals

How do great ideas come to fruition? Do you sit in a bathtub and exclaim, “eureka!” as loud as you can until something comes to mind?

There must be a better way!! In the previous Detour issues, we discussed the crucial first steps; completing a needs finding process. And once you’ve identified a need, then it’s time to get together and find a solution right? Right??

Every good idea needs a strong foundation to be built on, and that means you and your team need to be up to date on the topic at hand. That’s right, more research! Yay! And so begins the Needs Screening process which starts by identifying and understanding the Disease state fundamentals.

What are disease state fundamentals? Here are six aspects that are essential to ensuring you successfully build foundational knowledge regarding the disease you are addressing with your soon-to-be solution:

  1. Epidemiology: Population statistics addressing the causes, distribution, and control of disease.

  2. Anatomy and physiology: The structure and function of the organ system(s) in question in a healthy individual.

  3. Pathophysiology: The deviation from normal anatomy and physiology caused by a disease or other abnormality.

  4. Clinical presentation:The typical patient state when they present in a clinical setting, specifically the symptoms and signs of the disease.

  5. Clinical outcomes: The most common outcomes experienced by patients as a result of having the disease.

  6. Economic impact: Outlines the cost of the disease to the healthcare system, which will be important for determining the value of your technology/idea.

Once you have all this information and you put together a nice summary to share with the team and stakeholders, you are ready to move on. Just remember, a disease state is a dynamic thing that changes with every new bit of information discovered - which means your disease state fundamentals should be updated accordingly!

TLDR; before you can brainstorm a solution for your identified need, you have to read up on the disease; specifically the epidemiology, anatomy, physiology, pathophysiology, clinical presentation, clinical outcomes, and the economic impact.
DEEP DIVE

This week's deep dive looks at filters!

...No, not that type of filter. We are talking about the type used to isolate and purify cell cultures! STEMCELL Technologies is a Canadian biotechnology company headquartered right here in Vancouver.  Their work stems in developing technologies that assist with cell analysis including cell culture analysis machines and machines that assist with cell isolation.

Despite their success, STEMCELL is not alone. This sector of biotechnological innovation is increasingly prevalent, as cell type isolation is becoming more important.  A study by Liwski et al. indicates that sample purity is important for detecting weak donor specific antibodies which impact crossmatch results in organ donation.  Crossmatch screenings are used to indicate the likelihood of rejecting an organ in an organ transplant.

BREAK ROOM BANTS

Effective collaboration is all you need!

If you are in a coffee shop with a friend, and they gaze into your eyes to say, “My heart is full,” you might be happy that your friend is content with their life. If they say it to you in a clinic, you might be worried they have Congestive Heart Failure. Luckily, in the latter case, Dr. Yair Feld is stepping in to help.

Dr. Feld, a Toronto based cardiologist, took it upon himself to address a problem he saw building up in the treatment of congestive heart failure. He partnered up with a biomedical engineer in Israel and showed the potential of interdisciplinary collaboration. Together, they started Paragate Medical, a tech company that was selected as one of MedTechs Top 50 Global Innovators! 

Congestive Heart Failure is a non-curable, chronic condition, and symptom management is the mainstay of treatment. Nearly 4000 Americans were waiting for a heart transplant because of this disease in 2018. The main issue is that fluid builds up throughout the patients’ body. Paragate has designed an absorptive device that can be laparoscopically placed. It applies a pressure gradient causing fluid to be continuously drained by the urinary system. 

Dr. Feld created a device that benefits every stakeholder: patients have less burden from diuretics and improved clinical outcomes by preventing CHF exacerbations, physicians have an alternate solution to diuretic-resistant cases and the ability to manage fluid-overload from home, and the hospital has reduced rehospitalizations.
